Math Modeling

Math modeling is taught by Mr. Regele. In the class, we are currently working on a problem relating to perfect shuffles (Faro shuffles), which "involves splitting the deck into two equal halves of 26 cards and then interweaving them perfectly so that the cards from each packet alternate, coming together like a zipper." We are working on figuring out a formula to determine how many perfect shuffles it will take for a certain set of cards to return to its original state.

We are also learning how to use the Math Modeling program Wolfram Mathematica, which we will use for future math problems and competitions.
