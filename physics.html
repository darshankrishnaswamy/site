Physics

Physics at Mass Academy is taught by Mr. Ellis. So far, we have learned both 1-dimensional and 2-dimensional kinematics (the study of objects in motion), Forces, Work and Energy, Rotational Motion, and Rotational Dynamics.

We sometimes get a single advanced problem, called an Über problem. These problems have multiple parts and we get about a week to solve the problems and do a full write-up. They are very similar to lab write-ups, but we don't do the experiments ourselves.

Below is an Über problem that I did in October, where I was given a jumper sitting on an elevated surface, attached with a chain to a barrel of rocks. The task was to find the coefficient of friction between the jumper and the floor based on how far the jumper ended up from its starting point.

Here is another Über problem that I did in September. The question was about a model rocket which was launched vertically upward, and after it fell a certain distance from its maximum height, a parachute opened and the rocket approached a terminal velocity. The task was to find the total time that the rocket spent in the air based on this information.
